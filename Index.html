8 Limbs of Yoga

Our Gallery

"The 8 limbs of yoga offer a holistic path to spiritual and personal growth, integrating ethical principles, physical practices, and meditative techniques."

"Yama, the first limb of yoga, is crucial as it sets the foundation for ethical living, guiding us to live harmoniously with others and ourselves."

Yama Pose

"Niyama, the second limb of yoga, emphasizes personal discipline and self-care, fostering inner growth and a deeper connection to oneself."

Niyama Pose

Asana, the third limb of yoga, focuses on physical postures that build strength, flexibility, and balance, preparing the body for meditation and promoting overall well-being.

Asana Pose

"Pranayama, the practice of controlled breathing, enhances vitality and mental clarity by regulating the flow of life force energy within the body."

Prayama Pose

"Dharana, or concentration, is vital as it trains the mind to focus deeply, paving the way for profound meditation and inner stillness."

Dharana Pose

"Pratyahara is a practice of retreating from external distractions to turn the focus inward, enhancing self-awareness and deeper meditation."

Pratyahara pose

"Dhyana is the practice of focused concentration, training the mind to maintain unwavering attention on a single point or object."

Dhyana Pose

Samadhi, the final limb of yoga, represents the ultimate state of enlightenment and union, where the practitioner experiences profound peace and oneness with the universe."

Samadhi pose
